puppetize.net

Collaborative infrastructure development, self-learning and teaching.

Our Goals

The @puppetize organization on GitHub was founded in 2012 by me (@ustuehler) as an experiment in improving the development and adoption of shared modules and best practices for Puppet.

Our work here may overlap with example42 and other community projects, but rather than compete, we wish to learn from them and to collaborate as much as we can.

Beyond that, our ambitious aim is to build a cool self-teaching environment for beginners and advanced users alike, with basic online tutorials that can be executed in a browser, and with sandboxes hosted in an OpenStack private cloud for advanced learning. Think of tryruby.org, only for Puppet. :)

We also want to encourage the discussion of a wide range of meta-topics, including best practices regarding the setup of Puppet infrastructures, design principles for cross-platform modules, abstractions of basic infrastructure elements (like backup and monitoring solutions) and workflows for large teams.

Current Projects

The infrastructure project focuses on building the OpenStack private cloud that will host the online tutorial application and eventually the whole of puppetize.net. It builds on the OpenStack modules developed by Dan Bode (@bodepd) at PuppetLabs and includes Vagrant boxes and RSpec definitions for test-driven development. It's all on GitHub, so check it out and contribute!
